Publications

  • 2024.02.20
    Longitudinal monitoring of the mouse brain reveals heterogenous network trajectories during aging
    Communications Biology
    A longitudinal rs-fMRI study in mice revealed sex-dependent trajectories of brain network reorganization during aging, showing patterns of de-differentiation in females and increased somatomotor connectivity in males.
  • 2023.07.25
    The effects of sexual abuse on female adolescent brain structures
    Scandinavian Journal of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry and Psychology
    This study found that sexually abused adolescent females had structural brain changes in the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex, suggesting neurobiological alterations even in the absence of psychiatric diagnoses.
  • 2022.11.09
    Learning-related contraction of gray matter in rodent sensorimotor cortex is associated with adaptive myelination
    eLife
    Longitudinal VBM and immunohistochemistry in mice demonstrated that learning induces dynamic intracortical myelination, correlating with nonlinear changes in gray and white matter volumes.
  • 2022.01.24
    The Aged Striatum: Evidence of Molecular and Structural Changes Using a Longitudinal Multimodal Approach in Mice
    Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience
    Using PET and MRI, this study showed that aging mice exhibit decreased striatal D2 receptor availability and increased gray matter density, reflecting similar patterns seen in human aging.
  • 2021.07.30
    A longitudinal study of lateral ventricle volumes in deficit and non-deficit schizophrenia
    Psychiatry Research: Neuroimaging
    Over a five-year period, lateral ventricles expanded more in deficit than in non-deficit schizophrenia patients, suggesting differing trajectories of structural brain change.
  • 2021.04.01
    Brain areas associated with resilience to depression in high-risk young women
    Brain Structure and Function
    Young women resilient to depression showed increased volume and thickness in salience and limbic network regions, highlighting potential neural correlates of resilience.
  • 2020.03.01
    Testing an Internet-Based Turkish Obesity Behavioral Therapy Program: A Randomized Study
    Turkish Journal of Psychiatry
    A web-based cognitive behavioral therapy program led to significantly greater short-term weight loss compared to email education among Turkish overweight individuals.
  • 2020.02.01
    The negative association between amygdala volume and harm avoidance trait in healthy young women with a history of familial depression
    Asian Journal of Psychiatry
    In healthy women with familial depression, smaller amygdala volume correlated with higher harm avoidance, suggesting a neuroanatomical link to personality traits.
  • 2016.09.01
    Investigating Connectivity of Orbitomedial Prefrontal Region in a Patient with Traumatic Brain Injury
    Turkish Journal of Psychiatry
    A case study using DTI tractography revealed disrupted white matter connectivity in the orbitomedial prefrontal cortex following traumatic brain injury, highlighting the role of white matter damage in post-TBI symptoms.
  • 2016.06.30
    Cortical thickness and VBM in young women at risk for familial depression and their depressed mothers with positive family history
    Psychiatry Research: Neuroimaging
    Both high-risk daughters and their depressed mothers showed similar structural differences in temporoparietal and prefrontal regions, supporting these as potential trait markers for depression.
  • 2013.04.05
    The relationship between the anterior corpus callosum size and prefrontal cortex volume in drug-free depressed patients
    Journal of Affective Disorders
    The study found that depressed patients have reduced prefrontal cortex volume and a disrupted correlation with anterior corpus callosum size, indicating impaired structural connectivity.
  • 2012.02.01
    Reduced left uncinate fasciculus fractional anisotropy in deficit schizophrenia but not in non-deficit schizophrenia
    Psychiatry and Clinical Neurosciences
    DTI analysis showed that deficit schizophrenia patients had lower fractional anisotropy in the left uncinate fasciculus, suggesting specific frontotemporal white matter alterations.
  • 2010.09.01
    Small frontal gray matter volume in first-episode depression patients
    Turkish Journal of Psychiatry
    First-episode, drug-naive depressed patients exhibited reduced frontal gray matter volume, supporting the presence of neurostructural changes early in the course of depression.
